Classification Decision tree of chronic kidney disease


Introduction

Chronic kidney disease (CKD) is a condition where kidney function gradually declines over months or years. Initially, it has no specific symptoms, and it's detected by increased serum creatinine or protein levels. As CKD progresses, it can lead to elevated blood pressure, blood urea, sodium, potassium, and other indicators due to reduced kidney function. In our lab, we have 400 patients, and we've collected data on 24 features to examine their relationship with CKD. These features include blood pressure, specific gravity, albumin, sugar, red blood cell count, and others. We'll analyze this relationship using decision tree algorithms like C4.5 and CART, among others.
